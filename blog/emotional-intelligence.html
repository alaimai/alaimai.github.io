What I learned about emotional-intelligence

Blog-04 on 16th Mar, 2024

Hello everyone, welcome to my exploration of emotional intelligence. Firstly, I'd like to discuss why emotional intelligence matters. It's crucial because it affects how we interact with others, make decisions, and handle various situations in life. By developing emotional intelligence, we can better understand ourselves, communicate effectively, and navigate life's challenges more successfully. Throughout this journey, we'll delve into the importance of emotional intelligence and its impact on our daily lives. Let's embark on this enlightening journey together!

Emotional Intelligence as known as Emotional Quotient (EQ). It refers to a person's ability to recognize, understand, manage, and express emotions effectively, both in oneself and in others. EQ encompasses various skills, including empathy, self-awareness, self-regulation, social skills, and motivation. IQ, or Intelligence Quotient, is a measure of a person's cognitive abilities, including reasoning, problem-solving, memory, and comprehension skills. It's typically assessed through standardized tests and is used to gauge an individual's intellectual potential or capacity to learn.

Not only does IQ capture one aspect of intelligence, but emotional intelligence (EQ) also plays a significant role in it. EQ affects how we navigate social interactions, handle stress, make decisions, and form relationships. For instance, I felt very stressed when dealing with family matters and Sprint 3 together. Learning mindfulness and meditation has truly opened up a new world for me, bringing me a sense of peace and tranquility. IQ remains relatively stable throughout life, EQ can be developed and improved over time through self-reflection, practice, and learning. Cultivating emotional intelligence is essential in an individual's overall success and well-being.
